Writing Perspective

2023-07-20 - Written by: Filippo

The leading

Writing is tough. Imagine the heavy work of a logger. It is repetitive and dull. But is blogging that hard?

Probably not. However, the logger’s effort can be more effective with the right approach. The tree falls predictably, the logger work can be less arduous, and the logger avoids causing accidents. The same is true for the writer. The writer can transform his effort into significant work with the right approach. So, which is the most effective approach?

Making sure it is the right approach

Writing approaches can be judged by considering different factors. They prove effective when they achieve the desired outcomes. Bloggers’ desired result is to have readers. Especially when readers enjoy the content of their writing. So, the most crucial factor for writers is the reader. The writer has no purpose if there are no readers. The message is lost. *

It is interesting to highlight how this factor drastically separates high school writing from writing to sell (like journalists or bloggers). Since there is a big difference in approach, I want to provide a helpful perspective to new bloggers. It is a way of contributing to the blogging community. Blogs gave me valuable tools and ideas. I’m grateful. I want to give back.

Students

The student reader is the professor. The professor reads the student’s work and gives a mark. The professor is paid to grade. But he earns independently from the student’s ability. Instead, no reader is paid to read the blogger’s work.

Metamorphosis

A blogger’s ability is measured by how many readers read his work. It is also measured by readers’ responses: how much they appreciate his work. The student already has his reader - the professor. He has to complete his homework task. The writer has no readers to start with and no duty assigned.

To become a blogger, you are responsible for reaching out to potential readers. No one will magically start reading your work. Therefore, understand who the potential readers are. Find them and attract them. The blogger is also responsible for providing value. The reader expects something in return for his time and attention. It would be an uneven trade to give nothing to the reader.

Reaching Out

One way to attract readers is by promising value. Do not make exaggerated promises. Always make sure to deliver your promises. Do not fool readers by promising and not providing. Treating readers as fools is not wise. People remember that they were deceived and won’t trust you anymore. They will also alert others that you were deceptive. You will run out of readers and have a bad reputation.

Providing Value

The author can provide value in different ways. They can provide value as new knowledge for the reader or as time-saving.

The ending

The student and the blogger should adapt to their individual needs. They should use two different writing styles. I’m not suggesting that every student should become a blogger. When you are a student, use an approach that suits your situation. If you want to become a blogger, be aware of the crucial shift in perspective needed.

Resources

The fundamental skills to be a successful blogger are: attracting readers, providing value, and writing skills. Students may not have these skills. Here are some of the resources I used when I started writing. I hope they can be beneficial to anyone starting to write.

  • The Craft of Writing Effectively
  • On Writing Well: The Classic Guide to Writing Nonfiction - William Zinsser
