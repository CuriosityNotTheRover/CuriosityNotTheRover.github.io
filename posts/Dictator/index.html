Dictator Game

2024-01-20 - Written by: Filippo

Play this simulation game with me. Let’s pretend I will be the next Italian dictator.

Like in Welcome Mr. President. A film where fortuitous events led a librarian to become Italy’s president. Pretend I end up being a dictator. Even if it happens because of a mistake, I am in charge.

Should I resign as soon as possible, acknowledging the mistake? Probably yes. But I feel tempted. I am fuelling my imagination and thinking about this scenario daily. I’d love to do at least one good, lasting reform before withdrawing from office.

I genuinely care about society and its functioning. This simulation game/thought experiment is a way of assessing my ideas. Sharing them is also a way to receive feedback and suggestions. I am proposing ideas with Italy’s best interest in mind. In the game, I have the luxury of having authority and no worries about my popularity. I don’t have to stress that my changes won’t appeal to the public. I don’t need to be elected again. I can suggest whatever I believe Italy needs the most.

The pill prescription will be clear once you understand what I think plagues Italy. Italians are careless. They are not bothered by the consequences of their actions. Their action may destroy an entire city, and they would still sleep well at night. They only worry if something is a direct threat to them. They get started in no time. In Italy, everyone lives according to one motto: mind your own business. And everyone faithfully lives according to it. No wonder we have the mafia.

Anyway, this way of living is getting out of hand. Companies offering public services are a complete torture. Public companies’ profits are guaranteed even if their service is trash. Thus, managers and workers work carelessly, only worrying that their salary is deposited regularly. The consequences are disastrous. The poor quality of their services doesn’t bother them; workers put little effort into fixing problems. The insufficient quality is certified by the fact that they often don’t buy or use the products and services of their own company.

My prescription to end these problems is simple: publish a number. Which number? How is a number supposed to change such a phenomenon?

As a dictator, I want each public company to publish the hours head managers spend using the products/services they provide. That’s the number that should be public. People should be aware of how much the people responsible for the company are invested in it.

This idea came to my mind when taking public transport. The trains are a disaster in Milan’s area. I wondered how people responsible for the services could be so blind to the problems. I realized that they may not even use their services at all. Just take the profits from people using their services and travel by car.

If the people producing a pill are not willing to take it when they are sick, I am not taking it either. Why do you prefer your competitor to your product? Then I assume that you are doing something shady to profit from me. If someone builds a plane they are not taking, I am not taking it either. Again, the only assumption possible is that something shady is going on.

Profiting is not inherently wrong or evil. It becomes bad when the company does not provide value with its services and products. So, profit comes from people going down rather than progress. This time, a company is profiting off me taking trains so they can go to work by car. The fact that they work on the train lines is even more ludicrous.
