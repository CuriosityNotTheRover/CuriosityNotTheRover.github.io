How am I perceived by friends?

I am grateful to my closest friends. I ask them questions about me, and they answer. Nothing unexpected until here. The beauty of this simple exchange resides in the fact that they are honest (because they are real friends). They are also different from me. Seeing me from the outside gives them a different perspective on who I am, how I behave, and lots of small interesting things that sometimes I don’t even reflect on.

The kind of questions I asked: Have I behaved this way recently? Is this behaviour showing frequently? Have I improved on this thing recently? What things trigger some of my behaviour? Their answers often surprise me. Knowing them, I expect some answers but receive completely different answers. These questions always lead to a frank conversation if you listen without attitude. Thanks to my friends, I have a clear idea of what I could do differently.

I cannot perceive myself from the exterior or find a friend that is my replica. These two things create this unique situation. I can't experience myself from the outside and I can't even search for someone that is like me to tell me how I am. I am at peace with this fact even if the desire to see me from the outside remains inconceivable. All of this gives friends priceless importance.

Sometimes I feel that the question I ask leave them perplexed. It may seem that I obsess too much about how I am perceived. I am genuinely interested to hear things that I cannot experience directly, and how my behaviour is perceived is one of them.

I want to stress (because I want this article to be helpful) the importance of having good sincere friends and asking them for feedback and bits of advice. You can discover your blind spots.
